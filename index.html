What you're feeling

That heaviness
has a name.

When you seek outward — when you're chasing approval, comparing yourself, trying to prove your worth — your body knows.

You feel it as anxiety, tightness, fear, exhaustion, never-enough.

That feeling isn't a flaw in you. It's a signal. Your body is telling you: you're looking in the wrong direction. The thing you're chasing can't satisfy you.

What waiting means

Wait on the Lord.

Waiting isn't doing nothing. The Hebrew word for wait — qavah — means to bind yourself to, to hope in, to trust expectantly.

You wait on the Lord by depending on Him. By orienting yourself to Him. By saying with your whole life: I am yours, and what comes, comes from you.

And then something happens that you can't make happen on your own.

"Even youths shall faint and be weary,
and young men shall fall exhausted;
but they who wait for the Lord
shall renew their strength;
they shall mount up with wings like eagles;
they shall run and not be weary;
they shall walk and not faint."

Isaiah 40:30-31

You have been weary because you've been waiting on the world.

Wait on Him instead. The renewal is not far. It comes when the waiting changes direction.
